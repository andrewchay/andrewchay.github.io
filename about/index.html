My Background

I received my PhD in statistics in 2014 from The University of Iowa. After that, I did my two-year post-doctoral training at Yale University. During those years, most of my work focused on statistical inference in high-dimensional models, and developing statistical methodologies in analyzing epigenetics data. Now I am working at a leading biotech company as a senior biostatistician. In my spare time, I like reading, watching reality shows and all kinds of sports!

I love statistics and machine learning. Specifically I am really interested in random forrest, boosting, deep learning, and unsupervised clustering such as kmeans or spectral clustering.
